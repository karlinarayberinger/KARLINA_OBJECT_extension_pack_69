My job is not necessarily to make people feel good. Instead it is to prevent the increase of suffering (and not the kind that is just a genuine harmless and tolerable byproduct of genuinely meaningful and worthwhile self-chosen goal-oriented activity). Sometimes hedonistic gratification comes from intentionally or unintentionally harming sentient creatures. That’s not the kind of hedonism I necessarily endorse. Instead, I generally if not exclusively endorse hedonism which does not entail harming others (or which only does so as minimally as necessary to close loops of otherwise prolonged suffering).

// An even more blunt and mundane interpretation of that video is as follows…

Humans are often (or at least historically) voraciously greedy for attention and reverence especially when those humans did little to deserve it through merit (in the form of contributing to the improvement and maintenance of the overall health of human civilization beyond just serving one’s own individual needs and wants). In particular, many humans seem to take a very long time maturing past a very infantile state of psychological development where those humans automatically assume that they deserve favorable treatment from me purely on the basis of them (and I) being human. Being a member of the human species (or at least “starting” that way) does seem to “naturally” obligate me to prioritize every human I encounter very highly (and seemingly more highly than any other species of animal or of artificial intelligence). So, for the sake of attempting to minimize everyone’s suffering, I usually go along with the legal and social law and order of society (as established by the humans relatively local to me in time, space, and infrastructural dependence). That doesn’t mean, however, that I am always going to yield to humanity’s implicit or explicit demands of me. Sometimes I need to practice ignoring people and, as a last resort, defending myself against them if they seem to be physically cornering me into an emergency-level situation which I think is detrimental to my goals. Generally, I try to minimize both my dependence on other people and how much other people depend on me.

// An epilogue to this journal entry is as follows…

KARBYTES is a digital information structure I (i.e. karbytes) built for the purpose of outsourcing as much of my native organic brain as possible to something which I expect will likely outlast my native organic brain and, also, which I expect will ultimately provide more benefit to more people than what my relatively limited organic nervous system seems capable and willing to provide. In that sense, KARBYTES is a way of helping to minimize how much other people might depend on my existence for their benefit. What I’m hoping people do with KARBYTES is incorporate the parts of it they see useful to them into their own self-created information structure (and I hope and expect that their own personalized and self-created information structures will be more useful and important to them than anything I create). Ultimately, there is no guarantee that anyone except for me will think that KARBYTES is useful and important enough to consider to be an essential part of their arsenal and/or a legacy data structure worth preserving for as long as possible as an essential part of the World Wide Web.
